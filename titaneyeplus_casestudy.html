TITAN EYEPLUS · CONSUMER RETAIL · 2021

Redefining the journey of an online spectacle buyer

Titan Eyeplus is the largest eyewear retailer in India. We overhauled their digital experience to bridge the gap between their premium physical stores and their previously disjointed online presence, creating a unified phygital journey.

UX Design E-commerce Omnichannel
titaneyeplus.com/collection
The Context In twelve years, Titan Eyeplus grew to dominate the Indian offline eyewear industry. However, even after years of rapid retail expansion, their mobile and web traffic failed to reflect the stature of the brand. The software felt outdated, the purchasing flow was convoluted, and the trust necessary to buy prescription lenses online was completely absent. We were brought in to completely rethink the e-commerce portal from the ground up.

CHAPTER 01 · THE RETAIL DISCONNECT

A stark contrast in brand perception

Imagine stepping into a beautifully designed Titan Eyeplus physical store. You are immediately surrounded by sleek displays, expert optometrist advice, and an overriding feeling of refined quality.

Now, imagine experiencing the old online store. The contrast was jarring. Users were greeted by cluttered UI, confusing navigation, and an aesthetic that failed to communicate the premium nature of the brand.

Our mission wasn't simply to "improve" the website; it was to successfully translate the essence of the in-store experience into the digital realm. Every interaction needed to project trust, premium quality, and modern sophistication.

"The digital experience must be an extension of our flagship stores. Right now, we are diluting trust the moment a user lands online."

CHAPTER 02 · USER RESEARCH

Understanding the eyes behind the screens

We immersed ourselves in understanding the specific behavioral patterns of online eyewear shoppers. Buying spectacles is inherently high-friction: it requires a medical prescription, precise fit, and a significant aesthetic commitment.

Through our discovery phase, we mapped our users into two dominant archetypes, each requiring a fundamentally different journey to conversion.

Archetype Mapping: Priya (The methodical, prescription-driven buyer) and Ravi (The trend-conscious, exploratory buyer).

The central challenge: Design an architecture that allows Priya to efficiently upload her prescription and find exact replacements, while providing Ravi with rich discovery tools and virtual try-ons.

CHAPTER 03 · THE CORE JOURNEY

From confusion to absolute confidence

We mapped the entire customer journey, treating the architecture not as a set of pages, but as an ascending narrative of trust. We broke the core flow down into four distinct acts.

Act I: The First Impression (Homepage)

This was our chance to immediately establish the new brand identity. We deployed a clean, modern grid that echoed the in-store aesthetic. The flow became category and browsing-driven, eliminating the cognitive overload of the previous mega-menus. Large, high-fidelity hero imagery set the tone.

titaneyeplus.com

Act II: The Exploration (Catalog & Filters)

Spectacle catalogs are massive. We simplified the navigation by creating highly intuitive categories and a powerful, contextual filtering engine (shape, material, brand, price). We increased the size of the product thumbnails and ensured consistent, clinical photography to highlight hardware details.

titaneyeplus.com/eyeglasses

Act III: The Decision (Product Detail Page)

The PDP is where trust is either won or lost. We focused on providing all the technical specifications a customer needed: frame dimensions, material weight, and lens compatibility. We emphasized trust signals like return policies, secure payment options, and clear prescription upload instructions.

titaneyeplus.com/eyeglasses/titan-rimless

Act IV: The Commitment (Checkout)

A streamlined, secure, and user-friendly checkout process. We drastically reduced the number of form fields, offered persistent order summaries, and elegantly separated the lens selection logic from the payment logic to reduce friction.

titaneyeplus.com/checkout/secure
CHAPTER 04 · FEATURE HIGHLIGHT

Closing the physical gap with Virtual Try-On

The single biggest barrier to buying glasses online is the inability to answer: "How will this actually look on my face?"

To directly address this, we integrated an advanced AR Virtual Try-On (VTO) feature natively into the web experience. No apps required. Users could instantly overlay 3D models of frames onto their live camera feed, drastically reducing purchase anxiety and significantly lowering return rates.

CHAPTER 05 · DESIGN SYSTEM

Scalable foundations for omnichannel growth

To ensure long-term consistency across the web, mobile app, and in-store kiosks, we built a comprehensive design system. Every element, from typography and color semantics to complex specialized components (like prescription configuration modals), was rigorously documented and tokenized.
