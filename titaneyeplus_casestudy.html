TITAN EYEPLUS · CONSUMER RETAIL · 2021

Redefining the journey of an online spectacle buyer

Titan Eyeplus is India's largest eyewear chain — 850+ stores, 1 crore+ eyewear sold. Their digital experience didn't come close to reflecting that. We rearchitected the web and mobile product from the homepage to checkout, designing for the unique trust challenges of buying prescription eyewear online.

UX Design E-commerce Omnichannel
titaneyeplus.com
The Context In twelve years, Titan Eyeplus grew to dominate the Indian offline eyewear industry. However, even after years of rapid retail expansion, their mobile and web traffic failed to reflect the stature of the brand. The software felt outdated, the purchasing flow was convoluted, and the trust necessary to buy prescription lenses online was completely absent. We were brought in to completely rethink the e-commerce portal from the ground up.

CHAPTER 01 · THE RETAIL DISCONNECT

A stark contrast in brand perception

Imagine stepping into a beautifully designed Titan Eyeplus physical store. You are immediately surrounded by sleek displays, expert optometrist advice, and an overriding feeling of refined quality.

Now, imagine experiencing the old online store. The contrast was jarring. Users were greeted by cluttered UI, confusing navigation, and an aesthetic that failed to communicate the premium nature of the brand.

Our mission wasn't simply to "improve" the website; it was to successfully translate the essence of the in-store experience into the digital realm. Every interaction needed to project trust, premium quality, and modern sophistication.

"The digital experience must be an extension of our flagship stores. Right now, we are diluting trust the moment a user lands online."

CHAPTER 02 · USER RESEARCH

Understanding the eyes behind the screens

We immersed ourselves in understanding the specific behavioral patterns of online eyewear shoppers. Buying spectacles is inherently high-friction: it requires a medical prescription, precise fit, and a significant aesthetic commitment.

Through our discovery phase, we mapped our users into two dominant archetypes, each requiring a fundamentally different journey to conversion.

Archetype Mapping — Priya, 28: The hesitant first-timer who needs prescription confidence. Ravi, 35: The experienced trendsetter hunting new styles.

The central challenge: design one architecture that lets Priya efficiently upload her prescription and find exact replacements, while giving Ravi rich discovery tools and AI-powered frame recommendations.

CHAPTER 03 · THE CORE JOURNEY

From confusion to absolute confidence

We mapped the entire customer journey, treating the architecture not as a set of pages, but as an ascending narrative of trust. We broke the core flow down into four distinct acts.

Act I: The First Impression (Homepage)

The homepage was the new front door of the brand. We replaced the chaotic banner-and-coupon layout with a category-first grid that mirrored how customers browse in-store — by gender, frame type, and occasion. A persistent promotional hero strip, metric trust signals (1Cr+ eyewear sold, 850+ stores, 4.9 rating), and a "Frequently Bought" shelf surfaced discovery without cognitive overload.

titaneyeplus.com
Homepage redesign — category browsing, trust metric strip, and contextual promotional placements with designer annotations.

Act II: The Exploration (Catalog & Smart Filters)

Titan's catalog runs thousands of SKUs across multiple brands. The old filtered navigation was overwhelming and broke on mobile. We rebuilt it as a persistent left-rail filter system on desktop with contextually ordered parameters — Frame Style, Shape, Color, Size, Material, Brand, Price — alongside a mobile bottom-sheet equivalent. Thumbnail size increased by 40% and photography was standardised to a clean white background, letting frame hardware speak for itself.

titaneyeplus.com/eyeglasses/men
Catalog — persistent filter rail, standardised product photography, and the "Enable My Fit" AI layer surfaced inline.

Act III: The Decision (Product Detail Page)

The PDP is where trust is won or lost. We restructured the information hierarchy to answer the three questions a prescription buyer asks in order: Does it fit my face? Is it compatible with my prescription? Can I return it if it doesn't work? Frame dimension diagrams (bridge, temple, lens width) replaced vague size labels. Prescription lens compatibility was surfaced as a primary action — not buried in a footnote.

titaneyeplus.com/eyeglasses/fastrack-geometric-rimmed
PDP — frame dimension diagrams, inline colour picker, delivery date check, and a persistent "Buy Now" CTA.
Mobile comparison — old cart flow showing fragmented steps (left) vs. the redesigned PDP with inline lens selection and a persistent "Buy Now" CTA (right).

Act IV: The Commitment (Cart & Checkout)

The existing checkout was the single biggest drop-off point. Users hit three separate "apply coupon" dialogs before reaching payment. We consolidated the cart into one screen with all discount logic visible upfront — available coupons, loyalty points (Encircle, Tata Neu), and a clear itemised breakdown. The lens selection step was separated from payment to reduce cognitive load at the most critical moment.

CHAPTER 04 · FEATURE HIGHLIGHT

"Enable My Fit" — AI-powered frame matching

The biggest barrier to buying glasses online isn't price or trust — it's the inability to answer: "Will this shape actually work on my face?" Prescription buyers are especially risk-averse; a wrong frame means a wasted lens prescription.

To address this, we designed My Fit — an AI-powered recommendation layer built directly into the catalog. Rather than forcing users to navigate thousands of SKUs blind, toggling "Enable My Fit" surfaces only frames the algorithm predicts will complement the user's face geometry. The feature appears as a passive toggle within the existing browse flow — no separate app, no onboarding friction, no camera required. Just a smarter catalog.

"Enable My Fit" in the mobile catalog — AI filters the full product grid to frames suited to the user's face profile. No camera, no separate flow.
CHAPTER 05 · PROCESS

Wireframes first — validation before pixels

Before any visual design was committed, we mapped the entire system as low-fidelity wireflows — 20+ screens covering the homepage, catalog, PDP, lens selection, cart, checkout, and account flows. This let stakeholders stress-test the information architecture and prescription-upload logic without getting distracted by colour or branding debates.

Each screen was reviewed against the two primary archetypes: could Priya find the prescription-upload entry point within two taps? Could Ravi reach the My Fit toggle without losing his place in the browse flow? Flows that failed the two-tap test were restructured before any UI was built.
